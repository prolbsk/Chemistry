Lesson 7: Elements of Groups 16, 17 & 18

7.1 Introduction

In the p-block the differentiating electron enters the outermost p-orbital. A p-subshell holds up to six electrons → gives rise to Groups 13–18. ns² npˣ – last e⁻ in p-orbital.

7.2 Occurrence

  • Group 16 (chalcogens): O, S, Se, Te, Po. Oxides/sulfides form most metal ores. O₂ is 20.95 vol % of air; ~46 % of crust.
  • Group 17 (halogens): F, Cl, Br, I, At. Very reactive → exist as compounds (NaCl, sea-weed I, etc.).
  • Group 18 (noble gases): He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn. Monoatomic, inert; Ar ≈ 1 % of air; He from natural gas.

Chalcogens = ore formers; Halogens = salt born; Noble = inert.

7.3 Electronic Configuration

  • Group 16: ns² np⁴
  • Group 17: ns² np⁵
  • Group 18: ns² np⁶

Elements of G-16 & G-17 have 2 & 1 electrons fewer than the preceding noble gas.

ns² np⁴/⁵/⁶ – tells group.

7.4 Atomic & Physical Properties

7.4.1 Atomic Properties

  • Radii: ↓ group ↑ shells → radius ↑; across period → Zeff ↑ → radius ↓.
  • Ionization Enthalpy (IE): decreases down (size ↑), increases across (Zeff ↑).
  • Electronegativity (EN): decreases down; F (4.0) & O (3.5) highest.
  • Electron-Gain Enthalpy: less negative down; anomalies for O & F.

Size ↑ down → IE & EN ↓.

7.4.2 Physical Properties

  • G-16: O₂ gas; S, Se, Te solids; Po metallic & radioactive; all allotropes ↑.
  • G-17: F₂, Cl₂ gases; Br₂ liquid; I₂ solid; coloured; soluble in CS₂.
  • G-18: Monoatomic gases; very low m.p./b.p.; He 4.2 K; Ne 24.6 K; Ar 83.8 K.

Gases ↑ at top; solids ↓; noble gases monoatomic.

7.5 Anomalous Behaviour

Oxygen

  • Small size, high EN, no d-orbitals → O₂ paramagnetic; O₃ only oxide; H₂O liquid; covalency 2.
  • Other members show oxidation states +2, +4, +6.

O₂ odd one out.

Fluorine

  • Smallest atom, highest EN, no d-orbitals → weak F–F bond (lone-pair repulsion) → low BDE.
  • Only oxide OF₂; HF liquid (H-bonding); all reactions exothermic.

F anomaly: strongest EN, weakest F–F.

7.6 Chemical Properties

Oxidation States

  • G-16: –2 (common), +2, +4, +6 (stability of +6 ↓ down).
  • G-17: –1 (all); Cl, Br, I also +1,+3,+5,+7 (d-orbitals). F only –1.
  • G-18: 0; Xe shows +2,+4,+6 in fluorides/oxides.

More d-orbitals → higher oxidation states.

Reactions with H₂

  • G-16: H₂E (E=O→Te), sp³, angular; acidity H₂O < H₂S < H₂Se < H₂Te.
  • G-17: HX, strong acids; HF < HCl < HBr < HI.
  • G-18: Inert.

Acidity ↑ down group.

Reactions with O₂

  • G-16: EO₂ & EO₃ → acids (SO₂→H₂SO₃, SO₃→H₂SO₄).
  • G-17: Halogen oxides (Cl₂O, ClO₂, Br₂O…), unstable, strong oxidizers.
  • G-18: Inert.

Reactions with Halogens

  • G-16: EX₂, EX₄, EX₆ (SF₆ stable; SeF₆, TeF₆).
  • G-17: Interhalogens XX′, XX′₃, XX′₅, XX′₇ (e.g. ClF₃, IF₇).
  • G-18: XeF₂, XeF₄, XeF₆.

Interhalogen – more electroneg. diff → reactive.

Reactions with Metals

  • G-16: M + E → M–E (e.g. Mg + S → MgS).
  • G-17: M + X₂ → MX (ionicity MF > MCl > MBr > MI).
  • G-18: Inert.

7.7 Allotropy

Same element, different forms:

  • O₂/O₃
  • S: rhombic (α) & monoclinic (β)
  • Se: grey & red
  • Te: crystalline & amorphous
  • Po: α & β

Allotrope = same element, different form.

7.8 Oxoacids

Sulfur Oxoacids

  • H₂SO₃, H₂SO₄, H₂S₂O₇, H₂SO₅, H₂S₂O₈, H₂S₂O₃ (thio)
  • Higher oxidation → stronger acid

Halogen Oxoacids

  • Hypo- (HOX), halous (HOXO), halic (HOXO₂), perchloric (HOXO₃)
  • Strength: HClO < HClO₂ < HClO₃ < HClO₄

More O → stronger acid.

7.9 Oxygen & Its Compounds

Dioxygen (O₂)

  • Lab Prep: 2 KClO₃ → 2 KCl + 3 O₂ (MnO₂ cat.)
  • Industrial: fractional distillation of liquefied air
  • Properties: colourless, paramagnetic, bp 90 K, solubility 30.8 cm³/L (293 K)
  • Uses: respiration, steel-making, welding, rocket fuel

Simple Oxides

  • Acidic: CO₂, SO₂, N₂O₅ → acids
  • Basic: Na₂O, CaO → bases
  • Amphoteric: Al₂O₃
  • Neutral: CO, NO

Ozone (O₃)

  • Formation: O₂ + UV → O + O₂; O + O₂ → O₃
  • Lab Prep: silent discharge in O₂
  • Properties: pale-blue gas, strong oxidiser (“dry bleach”), bm 161 K, bent (117°)
  • Uses: bleaching, disinfection, air/water purification

Ozone umbrella.

7.10 Sulfur Compounds

Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂)

  • Prep: S + O₂ → SO₂; Na₂SO₃ + H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + SO₂ + H₂O
  • Properties: colourless, pungent, water-soluble → H₂SO₃; reducing agent
  • Uses: bleaching, preservative, H₂SO₄ manufacture

Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄)

  • Contact Process: S → SO₂ → SO₃ → H₂SO₄ (oleum dilution)
  • Properties: oily, m.p. 283 K, bp 611 K, strong dehydrating & oxidising agent
  • Uses: fertilizers, detergents, batteries, petroleum refining

7.11 Chlorine & Its Compounds

Chlorine (Cl₂)

  • Prep: MnO₂ + HCl; Deacon (HCl + O₂); brine electrolysis
  • Properties: greenish-yellow gas, 2–5× heavier than air, dissolves in H₂O → bleach
  • Uses: water sterilization, bleaching, PVC precursor

Hydrogen Chloride (HCl)

  • Prep: NaCl + H₂SO₄ → NaHSO₄ + HCl
  • Properties: colourless gas, bp 189 K, strong acid in H₂O (pK –7)
  • Reactions: NH₃ → NH₄Cl; aqua regia dissolves Au, Pt

7.12 Interhalogen Compounds

  • General formulae: XX′, XX′₃, XX′₅, XX′₇ (X = more electropositive)
  • Shapes: linear (ClF), T-shaped (ClF₃), square pyramidal (ClF₅), pentagonal bipyramidal (IF₇)
  • More electroneg. diff → weaker X–X′ bond → more reactive

7.13 Xenon Compounds

  • Xenon fluorides: XeF₂, XeF₄, XeF₆ (prep: Xe + F₂ under heat/pressure/discharge)
  • Oxyfluorides/oxides: XeO₃, XeOF₄, XeO₂F₂ (from hydrolysis of XeF₄)

Inert but can be coaxed to bond.
